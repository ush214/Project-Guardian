Mechanical Containment

Physically corralling oil to prevent spread.

Booms

Floating barriers used to contain/deflect slicks. Less effective in rough seas or strong currents.

Key Considerations

  • Effectiveness drops in waves >1 m or currents >1 knot.
  • Skilled personnel and vessels required.
  • Types: Containment, Sorbent, Fire (heat-resistant).

Best For

  • Protecting sensitive areas (shorelines, mangroves, intakes).
  • Concentrating oil for recovery.
  • Calm to moderate conditions.

Mechanical Recovery

Removing contained oil from the surface.

‚ñº

Skimmers

Devices that remove oil; most efficient on thick, contained slicks.

Key Considerations

  • Types: Oleophilic, Weir.
  • Efficiency depends on thickness and viscosity.
  • Water entrainment requires separation.

Best For

  • Large spills with booms in place.
  • Relatively calm sea states.

Sorbents

Absorbent materials (pads, booms, loose) useful for final cleanup or small slicks.

Key Considerations

  • Become hazardous waste when saturated.
  • Labor-intensive to deploy/recover.
  • Natural and synthetic options.

Best For

  • Thin sheens, small spills, hard-to-reach areas.

Chemical Methods

Changing oil properties using chemical agents.

‚ñº

Dispersants

Break oil into droplets to mix into the water column, accelerating natural breakdown.

Ecological Trade-Offs & Limitations

  • Moves oil into water column; impacts marine life.
  • Often restricted by regulations.
  • Effective only on fresh oil and certain conditions.

Best For

  • Very large spills in deep, open water.
  • When preventing shoreline impact is paramount.

Shoreline Cleanup

Removing oil that has reached the coast.

‚ñº

Manual Removal

Teams using rakes, shovels, sorbents. Effective but resource-intensive.

Key Considerations

  • Large workforce and logistics needed.
  • Lower ecological damage vs aggressive methods.
  • Generates substantial oiled solid waste.

Best For

  • Sensitive areas (marshes, mangroves).
  • Light to moderate oiling.

High-Pressure / Hot-Water Washing

Flushing oil from surfaces into water for collection by booms/skimmers.

Ecological Trade-Offs & Limitations

  • Highly destructive to surface biota.
  • May drive oil deeper into sediment.
  • Use sparingly with caution.

Best For

  • Man-made structures (piers, seawalls).
  • Less-sensitive rocky shorelines.
